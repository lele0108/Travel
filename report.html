introduction

When we were first tasked with creating a solution for migrating traditional media outlets to modern media techniques, we decided as a team to start from scratch. We completely ignored current media practices. Today, we're connected in thousands of different ways, for example, the internet, radio, word of mouth, and much more.

Media Examples:


Different media, coming in at different times. What's the last time you picked up a newspaper from the newspaper stand or your local coffee shop? We can't remember our last time. It's not a surprise to us that newspaper advertisement revenue has been freefalling since 2000, now less than half of what they earned a decade ago.


Uh oh, that's not good.


We've devised of a solution to disrupt media as it is today. Rise profits while lowering operating costs. We're in the 21st centurary, let's move forward and adapt a modern media strategy.

history of media



So what?


As you can see, traditional media distribution practices that are still used today come from as early as the 17th centurary. Wow. We've been relying on the same distribution of media for almost 5,000 years. There has to be a better way to do it now.


Let's change this.
